How this simulation works

You will make a series of decisions on compensation philosophy, pay structure, stakeholder negotiation, and crisis response. Every decision moves multiple dashboard metrics — usually some up and some down.

There is no perfect answer. Lead the market and you strain costs. Pay only for performance and you erode the loyalty of long-tenured staff. Choose transparency and you create new questions you must be ready to answer. Trade-offs are the point.

Throughout the simulation, any concept shown as a dotted red link can be tapped to open a definition card. The dashboard at the top of each stage shows the live state of eight organisational metrics.

At the end, you receive a personalised executive report — strengths, weaknesses, risks, badges earned, and an overall Strategic Compensation Score out of 100.

Stage 03 · Pay Structure Design

From philosophy to architecture.

Five structural decisions. Each carries operational consequence and signals philosophy in concrete terms.

Decision i — Grade Architecture
Structure
How many grade levels will NovaTech maintain across Analyst → Senior Analyst → Manager → Senior Manager → Director?
Broad Banding
3–4 wide bands. Career growth through skills, not titles.
↑ Flexibility · ↓ Hierarchy clarity
Moderate Grades
5–7 grades. Clear levels with reasonable progression.
= Balanced · = Conventional
Narrow Grades
10+ grades. Fine differentiation by role and tenure.
↑ Recognition · ↑ Pay compression

Spread

Narrow (±15%)
